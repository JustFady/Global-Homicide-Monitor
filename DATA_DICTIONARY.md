# 📖 Dataset Dictionary: Level 3 MBO & Options Microstructure

This dataset provides a nanosecond-level look into the underlying system architecture and physics engine of the financial market. It is specifically structured for high-frequency microstructure analysis, causal counterfactual modeling, and biomimetic visualization. 

The data is broken down into four core architectural components: State Coordinates, Global Telemetry, Queue Dynamics, and the Physics Engine (The Greeks).

---

## 📍 1. State Coordinates (System Time & Orchestration)
These variables define the exact node in the market structure where the data is being recorded. Think of these as the timestamped memory addresses or state coordinates for specific order book events.

* **`timestamp`**
  * **What it is:** The nanosecond-precision system clock time when the snapshot was generated by the exchange.
  * **Technical Role:** Crucial for synchronizing parallel data streams and orchestration. In a 3D visualization, this acts as the global clock dictating the rendering frame rate.
  * **Example:** `2025-04-22 15:47:00.237` (A market snapshot captured exactly at 3:47 PM and 237 milliseconds).
* **`Side`**
  * **What it is:** The direction of the transaction queue. `Ask` denotes the seller's queue; `Bid` denotes the buyer's queue.
  * **Technical Role:** Acts as a boolean filter determining whether the physical orders are creating resistance above the current price (`Ask`) or support below it (`Bid`).
  * **Example:** `Ask` (Sellers waiting in line, forming a "ceiling" above the current market price).
* **`future_strike`**
  * **What it is:** The specific, physical price level (in 0.25 tick increments) acting as the anchor point for the data row.
  * **Technical Role:** The absolute Y-coordinate in a 3D environment. Every single queue and calculation in the row executes strictly at this coordinate.
  * **Example:** `5362.25` (Traders have planted their limit orders exactly at this price tick).
* **`spx_strike`**
  * **What it is:** The corresponding target level for the underlying S&P 500 cash index.
  * **Technical Role:** Serves as the mapped coordinate to bridge the futures market back to the traditional stock market options grid.
  * **Example:** `5362` (The nearest whole-number anchor for the broader stock index).
* **`t` (Time to Expiration)**
  * **What it is:** The "Time-to-Live" (TTL) for the contract, represented as a continuous decimal of a year.
  * **Technical Role:** A hard countdown. As `t` approaches zero, the mathematical algorithms driving the market force the futures price to converge with the cash index price.
  * **Example:** `0.082` (Roughly 30 days remaining until this contract expires and ceases to exist).

## 🌍 2. Global Telemetry (The Macro State)
These variables represent the overall macro state of the system, acting as the global baseline rather than localized events at a specific strike.

* **`current_es_price`**
  * **What it is:** The live, active execution price of the E-mini S&P 500 futures contract at that exact nanosecond.
  * **Technical Role:** The system's current "center of mass." The distance between this variable and the `future_strike` dictates the load and sensitivity metrics. 
  * **Example:** `5286.75` (The exact price the last buyer and seller just agreed upon in the live market).
* **`spx_price`**
  * **What it is:** The computed ground-truth value of the 500 underlying stocks in the index.
  * **Technical Role:** The baseline shadow state. The futures price orbits this number, factoring in system overhead like interest rates.
  * **Example:** `5281.78` (The mathematical weight of the top 500 US companies at that exact second).

## 🧱 3. Queue Architecture (Level 3 MBO Microstructure)
Standard datasets only show total bandwidth (total volume at a price). This Market-By-Order (MBO) data unpacks the queue array to show the exact load distribution and individual packets (orders) waiting at the `future_strike`.

* **`MBO_pulling_stacking`**
  * **What it is:** The net delta of the order queue. It measures the volume of new orders injected into the system minus the volume of orders aggressively canceled before execution.
  * **Technical Role:** A critical indicator of systemic stability or spoofing (fake orders). Negative numbers indicate the queue is collapsing; positive indicate structural reinforcement.
  * **Example:** `-45` (Traders just yanked 45 contracts out of the queue out of fear, weakening the structure at this strike).
* **`MBO_1` through `MBO_14`**
  * **What it is:** The exact sizes of the first 14 individual positions waiting in the limit order queue at this `future_strike`.
  * **Technical Role:** Unmasks the structure of the liquidity load. 
  * **Example:** `MBO_1 = 500` (A massive institutional "whale" is first in line, acting as a major roadblock for the price).

## ⚙️ 4. The Physics Engine (Options Greeks)
The Greeks are the continuous partial derivatives used to calculate system risk. Every strike has two sides: **Calls** (upside mechanics) and **Puts** (downside/hedging mechanics). 

### First-Order Sensitivities (Direct Reactions)
* **`call_delta` & `put_delta` (Velocity)**
  * **What it is:** The rate of change of the option's value relative to a $1 move in the `current_es_price`.
  * **Technical Role:** The directional vector. Call delta ranges from 0 to 1; Put delta ranges from -1 to 0.
  * **Example:** `0.50` (If the S&P 500 moves up by $1, this option gains $0.50 in value).
* **`call_theta` & `put_theta` (Resource Decay)**
  * **What it is:** The daily bleed of the contract's value purely due to the passage of time.
  * **Technical Role:** A negative constant pressure acting on the system as expiration approaches.
  * **Example:** `-4.60` (This contract bleeds $4.60 in value every single day just sitting there).
* **`call_vega` & `put_vega` (Volatility Sensitivity)**
  * **What it is:** The contract's sensitivity to a 1% change in implied volatility.
  * **Technical Role:** Measures how the system reacts to chaos or uncertainty. 
  * **Example:** `6.05` (If the market panics and volatility spikes 1%, the option's value inflates by $6.05 instantly).
* **`call_rho` & `put_rho` (Macro Environment)**
  * **What it is:** The contract's sensitivity to a 1% change in the risk-free interest rate.
  * **Technical Role:** A slow-moving macro variable reflecting the cost of capital.
  * **Example:** `1.90` (A 1% hike in interest rates changes the contract value by $1.90).

### Second-Order Sensitivities (Acceleration & Complex Reflexes)
These track the rate of change of the First-Order metrics, acting as stress tests for the system's structural integrity.
* **`call_gamma` & `put_gamma` (Acceleration)**
  * **What it is:** The rate of change of the Delta. How much Delta increases for a $1 move in the underlying price.
  * **Technical Role:** The system's explosiveness. High Gamma creates violent feedback loops.
  * **Example:** `0.05` (For every $1 the market moves, the Delta gets 0.05 faster/stronger).
* **`call_vanna` & `put_vanna` (Delta vs. Volatility)**
  * **What it is:** Measures how much the Delta changes when Volatility (Vega) changes.
  * **Technical Role:** Maps how market panic physically alters the directional mechanics of the contract.
  * **Example:** `0.06` (A 1% jump in volatility shifts the directional Delta by 0.06).
* **`call_vomma` & `put_vomma` (Volatility Acceleration)**
  * **What it is:** The rate of change of Vega. Measures how sensitivity to volatility changes as volatility itself increases.
  * **Technical Role:** The "convexity" of fear.
  * **Example:** `1.29` (As the market gets wilder, the sensitivity to that wildness accelerates by 1.29).
* **`call_charm` & `put_charm` (Delta Decay)**
  * **What it is:** Measures how much the Delta changes as Time (`t`) passes.
  * **Technical Role:** Calculates how the directional exposure of the market ages over time.
  * **Example:** `-0.0004` (The directional speed of the option drops by a tiny fraction of a cent each passing day).
